Using Isaac to analyze genome sequencing data

by Niema Moshiri

  • Open the “Apps” tab in BaseSpace and select “Variant Calling Assessment Tool”.

  • Select version 2.3.0 and click “Launch” in the upper-right portion of the page.

  • Next to “Save Results To:”, click “Select Project(s):” and choose the “WGS vs. WES Results” project that you created earlier when executing the Isaac apps.

  • Under the table with columns “ID,” “Input VCF File,” and “Name,” next to “#1”, click “Select File(s):” and select “NA12878-TruSeqPCRFree-WGS_S1.vcf” (the output of the WGS Isaac app).

  • Click on the “+” symbol directly below the “#1” row, and in the new row that appears (“#2”), click “Select File(s):” and select “NA12878-NRC-Exome_S1.vcf” (the output of the WES Isaac app).

  • Specify the labels for #1 and #2 (e.g. WES and WGS or the filenames).

  • Lower on the page, check the box next to “Compute Pairwise Intersects:”

  • Next to “Filter Results by Region (optional),” check the box next to “Nextera Rapid Capture Exome v1.2”.

  • Check the box next to the acknowledgement at the bottom of the page (next to “BaseSpace Labs:”).

  • Click the “Continue” button at the top-right of the page to begin the execution of the app. This app takes approximately 40 minutes to finish running.
